## 摘要
歌曲中蕴含着什么？尽管音乐的演化一直是哲学和科学研究的主题，但通过音乐质量传递的适应性（fitness）的确切机制尚未得到研究。由于音乐和舞蹈在功能上旨在吸引观察者的注意力，我们研究了刺激观众注意力行为的声音特征。我们提出了一种视听注意力演算，用于确定个体的 f(x)=控制（Control）=位置、f1(x)=能量（Energy）=位置变化以及 f2(x)=惊喜（Surprise）=方向变化（简称 CES）。我们开发了相关理论和软件，用于：(A) 提取音频 CES；(B) 在 CES 的三元空间内动态映射音频轨迹；(C) 衡量表演者的稳定性（即质量）和歌曲的个性。我们验证了人类音乐以及人类/动物的发声在 CES 空间中都保持着高度的稳定性，其中专业音乐家的稳定性最高。CES 的稳定性还反映了人类和金丝雀在专家与新手之间歌唱能力的差异，并在现场观众互动过程中也施加了约束。我们的结论是，通过这种行为展示中常见的注意力演算进行的质量信号传递，反映了观察视听运动的物理学规律，这可能起源于埃迪卡拉纪-寒武纪边界后形成的种内/种间冲突的选择压力，并保留在今天的音乐中。

## Abstract
What is in a song? Although the evolution of music has been subject to philosophical and scientific study, the exact mechanism(s) of fitness signaled through the quality of music has not. As music and dance functionally aim to hold an observers attention, we investigate the features of sound that stimulate attentive behavior in an audience. We propose a calculus of audiovisual attention to determine an individuals f(x)=Control=position, f1(x)=Energy=change in position and f2(x)=Surprise=change in direction (CES). We develop theory and software to, (A) extract audio CES, (B) dynamically map audio trajectories within the ternary space of CES, and (C) measure performer stability (i.e. quality) and song individuality. We validate that both human music and human/animal vocalizations all maintain high stability in the space of CES, with professional musicians being the most stable. Stability of CES also reflects differences in expert vs. novice singing ability in both humans and canaries and imposes constraints during live audience interaction as well. We conclude that quality signaling via this calculus of attention common to behavioral display reflects the physics of observing audiovisual motion, probably originating from selective pressure of intra/interspecific conflicts formed after the Ediacaran-Cambrian boundary and retained in music today.
