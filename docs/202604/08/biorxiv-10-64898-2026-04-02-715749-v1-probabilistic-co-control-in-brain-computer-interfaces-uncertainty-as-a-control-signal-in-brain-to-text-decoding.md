## 摘要
目的：神经解码器在协同控制的脑机文本 BCI 中充当概率接口，其中预测的不确定性塑造了假设生成和语言模型集成，使得在不确定性下能够安全地做出决策。然而，目前尚不清楚这些解码器是否产生了可靠且具有信息量的不确定性，或者训练目标如何塑造这些特性。本研究对脑机文本解码中的不确定性表示进行了表征、诊断和改进。方法：我们扩展了两个指标，即校准误差 (ECE) 和分辨率 (RES)，以评估从帧级音素估计到词级假设的序列概率预测，从而量化模型不确定性的可靠性和信息量。利用该框架，我们分析了使用联结主义时间分类 (CTC) 训练的神经解码器。为了隔离独立于准确性的不确定性的因果作用，我们执行了 Oracle 干预，在保持预测序列固定的同时操纵预测的概率分布。受观察到的失效现象启发，我们进一步研究了训练目标的作用，并提出了一种两阶段交叉熵 (CE) 公式，将对齐推理与分类解耦。主要结果：我们表明，脑机文本 BCI 中广泛使用的 CTC 训练神经解码器会产生系统性的过度自信预测，即使在预测错误时也保持高置信度。Oracle 干预表明，改进的 ECE 和 RES 通过促进多样化的备选方案以及对符合用户意图的假设进行更有效的重排序，增强了假设生成和语言模型集成。从机制上讲，CTC 依赖过度自信的预测来解决对齐歧义。用 CE 损失取代 CTC 可以在不降低解码准确性的情况下，产生显著更可靠且更具信息量的概率预测。意义：不确定性成为脑机文本接口中的系统级设计变量。来自神经解码器的校准不确定性实现了与独立训练的语言模型的有效集成以及可靠的错误检测。本研究将不确定性从被动输出重新定义为主动控制信号，确定了概率协同控制的关键组件和评估标准，并勾勒出通往支持与世界进行日益复杂交互的下一代 BCI 的路径。

## Abstract
Objective: Neural decoders serve as probabilistic interfaces in co-control brain-to-text BCIs, where predicted uncertainty shapes hypothesis generation and language model integration, enabling decisions to be made safely under uncertainty. However, it remains unclear whether these decoders produce reliable and informative uncertainty, or how training objectives shape these properties. This work characterizes, diagnoses, and improves uncertainty representations in brain-to-text decoding. Approach: We extend two metrics, calibration error (ECE) and resolution (RES), to evaluate sequential probabilistic predictions from frame-level phoneme estimates to word-level hypotheses, quantifying the reliability and informativeness of model uncertainty. Using this framework, we analyze neural decoders trained with connectionist temporal classification (CTC). To isolate the causal role of uncertainty independent of accuracy, we perform oracle interventions that manipulate predicted probability distributions while holding predicted sequences fixed. Motivated by the observed failures, we further examine the role of the training objective and propose a two-stage cross-entropy (CE) formulation that decouples alignment inference from classification. Main Results: We show that widely used CTC-trained neural decoders in brain-to-text BCIs produce systematically over-confident predictions, with high confidence persisting even when predictions are incorrect. Oracle interventions reveal that improved ECE and RES enhance hypothesis generation and language-model integration by promoting diverse alternatives and more effective re-ranking of hypotheses aligned with user intent. Mechanistically, CTC relies on over-confident predictions to resolve alignment ambiguity. Replacing CTC with CE loss yields significantly more reliable and informative probabilistic predictions without degrading decoding accuracy. Significance: Uncertainty emerges as a system-level design variable in brain-to-text interfaces. Calibrated uncertainty from neural decoders enables effective integration with independently trained language models and reliable error detection. This work reframes uncertainty from a passive output into an active control signal, identifies key components and evaluation criteria for probabilistic co-control, and outlines a pathway toward next-generation BCIs that support increasingly complex interactions with the world.
