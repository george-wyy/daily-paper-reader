## 摘要
背景：AT(N) 生物学框架利用脑脊液（CSF）生物标志物对阿尔茨海默病（AD）病理进行分类，其中 A+T+ 分型定义为生物学意义上的 AD，而 A-T+ 分型则代表一种生物学上截然不同的实体，符合疑似非阿尔茨海默病病理生理学（如原发性高龄相关 tau 蛋白病）。目前能够以非侵入方式区分这些分型的功能性评估手段仍然有限。本研究探讨了在视觉 Oddball 任务期间，认知辐辏和瞳孔的时间动态特征是否能够区分轻度认知障碍（MCI）患者中的 A-T+ 与 A+T+ 生物学分型。方法：38 名经 CSF 生物标志物分类的 MCI 受试者（12 名 A-T+，26 名 A+T+）在连续眼动追踪下完成了视觉 Oddball 任务（80% 干扰刺激，20% 目标刺激）。采用线性混合效应模型分析了全时间序列及六个试次级时间特征上的“分型 × 条件”交互作用。在调整年龄、性别和 MMSE 评分后，使用二项逻辑回归评估了参与者层面的区分度。结果：两种分型在整体动眼反应幅度上表现相当，但在时间组织上存在显著差异。认知辐辏的全局斜率、达峰时间以及瞳孔达峰时间均表现出显著的“分型 × 条件”交互作用。逻辑回归证实，时间特征在参与者层面区分了生物学分型，且这种区分方向在干扰条件和目标条件之间发生了反转。此外，A-T+ 参与者保持了更高的目标检测准确率（89.3% vs. 82.4%, p = 0.001）。结论：Oddball 任务期间的认知辐辏和瞳孔时间动态提供了依赖于任务条件的功能性动眼特征，能够系统地区分 MCI 患者的 AT(N) 生物学分型。这表明动眼评估可能为基于 CSF 的分型鉴定提供一种便捷、非侵入性的补充手段。

## Abstract
BackgroundThe AT(N) biological framework classifies Alzheimers disease (AD) pathology using CSF biomarkers, with the A+T+ profile defining biological AD and the A-T+ profile representing a biologically distinct entity consistent with suspected non-Alzheimers pathophysiology, such as primary age-related tauopathy. Functional assessment capable of differentiating these profiles non-invasively remains limited. This study investigates whether cognitive vergence and pupillary temporal dynamics during a visual oddball task can distinguish A-T+ from A+T+ biological profiles in individuals with mild cognitive impairment (MCI).

MethodsThirty-eight participants with MCI (12 A-T+, 26 A+T+) classified by CSF biomarkers completed a visual oddball task (80% distractors, 20% targets) under continuous eye-tracking. Linear mixed-effects models examined profile x condition interactions on full time series and six trial-level temporal features. Participant-level differentiation was assessed using binomial logistic regression, adjusting for age, sex, and MMSE.

ResultsBoth profiles showed comparable overall oculomotor response magnitudes but diverged markedly in temporal organization. Significant profile x condition interactions emerged for cognitive vergence global slope, time to peak, and pupillary time to peak. Logistic regression confirmed that timing features discriminated biological profiles at the participant level, with differentiation reversing direction between distractor and target conditions. A-T+ participants also maintained superior target detection accuracy (89.3% vs. 82.4%, p = 0.001).

ConclusionCognitive Vergence and pupillary temporal dynamics during an oddball task provide condition-dependent functional oculomotor signatures that systematically differentiate AT(N) biological profiles in MCI, suggesting that oculomotor assessment may offer an accessible, non-invasive complement to CSF-based profile characterization.
