## 摘要
节律性采样是许多不同生物中众多感觉系统的显著特征。在灵长类视觉中，快速注视眼动（扫视）以 2-6 Hz 的频率持续扫描视觉环境，但扫视间间期分布的统计特性既可以用非节律性的随机过程来描述，也可以用（推测为可变的）节律性振荡器来描述。这提出了一个根本性的问题：灵长类的视觉扫描是否真的不同于跨物种和感觉模态所表现出的众多节律性采样策略示例。在本研究中，通过对人类、猕猴和普通狨猴进行的实验，并结合受神经元放电模式时间结构研究启发的统计方法，我们证明了灵长类扫视的产生明确地源于节律性机制。尽管这三种灵长类动物在进化上存在显著差异，但这一发现表现出了惊人的一致性。我们发现扫视率会经历平滑且缓慢的波动。考虑这些频率波动对于揭示扫视节律性的真实程度至关重要。因此，精确的扫视时机是由缓慢的扫视率波动与更具局部时间特性的节律性发生器之间的相互作用决定的。这种对显性眼动采样行为节律性的论证，为许多核心感知和认知过程的根本节律性提供了联系，并将灵长类扫视采样纳入了节律性主动采样行为的大家族中。

## Abstract
Rhythmic sampling is a hallmark of many sensory systems in many diverse organisms. In primate vision, rapid foveating eye movements continuously scan the visual environment at a rate of 2-6 Hz, but the statistics of inter-saccadic interval distributions could equally well be described by non-rhythmic stochastic processes as by a (putatively variable) rhythmic oscillator. This raises the fundamental question whether primate visual scanning actually differs from the numerous examples of rhythmic sampling strategies exhibited across species and sensory modalities. Here, using experiments in humans, macaques, and a marmoset, as well as statistical approaches inspired by studies of temporal structure in neuronal spiking patterns, we show that primate saccade generation is unambiguously rooted in a rhythmic source. This finding was remarkably consistent across the three primate species despite their significant evolutionary distance. We find that saccade rate undergoes smooth, slow fluctuations. Accounting for those rate fluctuations was crucial for revealing the true degree of saccade rhythmicity. Thus, exact saccade timing is determined by an interaction between the slow saccade-rate fluctuations with the more temporally-local rhythmic generator. This demonstration of rhythmicity in overt oculomotor sampling behavior provides a link to the fundamental rhythmic nature of many central perceptual and cognitive processes, and places primate saccadic sampling into the large family of rhythmic active sampling behaviors.
