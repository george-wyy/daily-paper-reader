## 摘要
当伸手抓取目标时，我们通常会在整个过程中将视线指向目标。如果在伸手过程中出现一个辅助性扫视目标，并且我们被指示将视线转向这个目标，那么视线转移通常会延迟到伸手目标被触及之后——这一现象被称为注视锚定。在这里，我们比较了人类参与者在伸手抓取视觉目标与视觉-触觉目标（接触时提供力反馈）时的注视锚定行为。我们还检查了双手情境下的注视锚定，参与者用右手伸手，并被指示在辅助目标出现时立即将视线转向该目标并移动左手。我们发现，在伸手运动过程中，对于视觉目标和视觉-触觉目标，视线都锚定在目标上。在视觉-触觉条件下，视线似乎在伸手的初始导向阶段（此时周边视觉引导手接近目标区域）被锚定。在视觉条件下，视线似乎在导向阶段和随后的引导阶段（此时中央视觉引导手）都被锚定。在双手伸手时，观察到注视锚定，但锚定并未扩展到左手，左手在眼睛开始移动之前就开始移动。总体而言，我们的研究结果表明，物体操控中眼和手运动的时间与目标注视的功能相关。

新意与亮点：当伸手抓取视觉目标时，即使出现一个竞争性扫视目标，视线通常也会锚定在目标上。我们显示，对于视觉-触觉目标也会发生锚定，但仅限于伸手的初始阶段，这可能是因为不需要视觉来确认目标触及。与视线不同，非抓取手没有观察到锚定，它可以在一出现竞争目标时就开始移动。

## Abstract
When reaching to a target, we typically direct our gaze to the target throughout the reach. If a secondary saccade target is presented during the reach, and we are instructed to shift our gaze to this target, the gaze shift is generally delayed until the reach target has been attained--a phenomenon known as gaze anchoring. Here, we compared gaze anchoring in human participants when reaching to a visual target versus a visual-haptic target providing force feedback upon contact. We also examined gaze anchoring in a bimanual context in which participants reached with their right hand and were instructed to both shift their gaze and move their left hand to the secondary target as soon as it appeared. We found that, during the reaching movement, gaze was anchored to the target for both visual and visual-haptic targets. In the visual-haptic condition, gaze appeared to be anchored during the initial directing phase of the reach during which peripheral vision directs the hand to the vicinity of the target. In the visual condition, gaze appeared to be anchored during both the directing phase and subsequent guiding phase during which central visual guides the hand. In two-handed reaching, gaze anchoring was observed but anchoring did not extend to the left hand, which started moving before the eyes. Overall, our findings indicate that the timing of eye and hand movements in object manipulation is linked to the function of target fixations.

New & NoteworthyWhen reaching to a visual target, gaze is typically anchored to the target even when a competing saccade target appears. We show that anchoring also occurs for visual-haptic targets but is limited to the initial phase of the reach, likely because vision is not required to confirm target attainment. In contrast to gaze, anchoring is not observed for the non-reaching hand, which can begin moving to a competing target as soon as it appears.
