## 摘要
眨眼是脑电图（EEG）中最大的生理伪迹之一，通常会在记录中被去除。然而，其时间点可能携带认知信息。本研究探讨自发眨眼在试次间的时间分布是否可以作为工作记忆中内部注意聚焦的时间分辨行为标志。实验1中，眨眼锁定的EEG分析显示，眨眼时间与反映对相关内部表征进行注意聚焦的神经活动相一致。实验2中，参与者在不同条件下记住相同的视觉信息，但相关项目要么在存储早期被提示，要么在记忆探测呈现时被延迟提示。眨眼频率分布相应变化：在早期选择条件下，提示后眨眼增加；在延迟选择条件下，探测后眨眼增加。早期选择条件下提示后的眨眼还与更好的记忆表现相关。因此，更广泛地说，自发眨眼可能为追踪潜在认知加工提供一种非侵入性的计时信号。

## Abstract
Eye blinks are among the largest physiological artefacts in electroencephalography and are typically removed from the recordings. Yet their timing may carry information about cognition. Here, we asked whether the temporal distribution of spontaneous blinks across trials provides a time-resolved behavioural signature of internal attentional focusing in working memory. In Experiment 1, blink-locked EEG analyses showed that blink timing was aligned with neural activity reflecting attentional focusing on a relevant internal representation. In Experiment 2, participants remembered the same visual information across conditions, but the relevant item was cued either early during storage, or later with presentation of the memory probe. Blink-frequency profiles shifted accordingly, increasing after the cue when selection was possible early and after the probe when selection was delayed. Post-cue blinks in the early-selection condition were also associated with better memory performance. Thus, more generally, spontaneous blinks might provide an unobtrusive chronometric signal for tracking latent cognitive processing.
