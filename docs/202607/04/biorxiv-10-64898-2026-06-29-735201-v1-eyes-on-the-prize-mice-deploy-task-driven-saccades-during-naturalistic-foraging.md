## 摘要
主动感知使生物能够通过自我产生的动作塑造传入的感觉信息，而扫视眼动是视觉中这一过程的关键读出示数。在灵长类动物中，扫视受到不确定性、价值和行为目标等认知变量的强烈调节，尤其是在复杂、自然的环境中。相比之下，在啮齿类动物中，扫视在很大程度上被解释为头眼协调的反射性组成部分，目标导向的视觉采样的证据仍然稀少。在这里，我们研究了小鼠在具有自然刺激的可穿越沉浸式虚拟环境中进行视觉依赖觅食任务时如何使用扫视。我们将其扫视动态与在虚拟环境中观察到的行为策略相关联。当可用到信息性感觉证据时，目标导向的扫视特别出现，但当动物能够依赖先前学习的空间偶然性时，也会预期性地发生，表明扫视不仅由即时视觉输入引导，还由内部表征引导。引人注目的是，扫视间隔的时间结构与先前在灵长类动物中报告的特征相似，并且在任务偶然性变化后随着处理需求增加而延长。总之，这些发现表明小鼠扫视不仅仅是反射性的注视校正，而是认知调节的主动采样策略的一部分。更广泛地说，它们表明主动视觉感知的关键原理可能在物种间保守，并确立了小鼠眼动行为作为内部认知状态的可处理读出示数。

## Abstract
Active sensing allows organisms to shape incoming sensory information through self-generated actions, and saccadic eye movements provide a key readout of this process in vision. In primates, saccades are strongly modulated by cognitive variables such as uncertainty, value, and behavioural goals, particularly in complex, naturalistic settings. In rodents, by contrast, saccades have largely been interpreted as reflexive components of head-eye coordination, and evidence for goal-directed visual sampling has remained sparse. Here, we examined how mice use saccades during a vision-dependent foraging task in a traversable immersive virtual environment with naturalistic stimuli. We correlated their saccade dynamics with behavioural strategies observed within the virtual environment. Target-directed saccades emerged specifically when informative sensory evidence was available, but also occurred anticipatorily when animals could rely on previously learned spatial contingencies, indicating that saccades were guided not only by immediate visual input but also by internal representations. Strikingly, the temporal structure of inter-saccade intervals resembled signatures previously reported in primates and lengthened with increased processing demand following changes in task contingencies. Together, these findings show that mouse saccades are not merely reflexive gaze corrections, but form part of a cognitively modulated active sampling strategy. More broadly, they suggest that key principles of active visual sensing may be conserved across species and establish mouse oculomotor behaviour as a tractable readout of internal cognitive state.
